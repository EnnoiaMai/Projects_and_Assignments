Art and Architecture

Montreal is home to many museums that provide background on its history. Here are some of the museums that exemplify the depth of Montreal's traditions, cultures, and environment. Montreal is also known for its unique sense of architecture as seen through the way its buildings were constructed: from French to Gothic styles.

Centre d'Histoire de Montreal

A museum showcasing art associated with Montreal's history. There are three floors of art exhibits.

Montreal Museum of Fine Arts (Musee des Beaux-Arts)

One of the most popular art museums in Montreal and in Canada. It is a large museum that spans across five pavilions.

Biodome

A facility that presents the ecosystems of North America. It is similar to an indoor zoo and there are many land and aquatic animals that reside there.

Montreal Biosphere

Designed by Buckminster Fuller, this site acts as a museum that targets awareness of environmental issues. Located at Parc Jean-Drapeau.

Olympic Park

The venue for the 1976 Summer Olympics. This place includes an observatory that presents a great view of the area.

Habitat 67

Designed by Mosche Safdie, it is a housing complex unique in its architecture. It was initially built for Expo 67, the World Fair held in that year. The structures and rooms are interlocked and laid atop one another positioned in different angular and perpendicular conformations.
