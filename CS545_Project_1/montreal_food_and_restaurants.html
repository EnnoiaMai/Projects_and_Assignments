Food and Restaurants

In the early 1900s, Montreal was strict to its French cuisines but due to recent changes in demographics due to immigration, the city began to incorporate more diverse dishes. Although there is a federal and provincial tax on foods at restaurants, the prices of these meals are relatively inexpensive. Canadian beer and Quebec cheese should also be explored.

Schwartz's

A famous smoked meat restaurant in Montreal. The restaurant is a deli established by Reuben Schwartz.

Poutineville

A restaurant that offers one of the most popular dishes in Canada, the poutine. Its ingredients include french fries, cheese, and gravy.

Il Miglio

A famous pasta restaurant in Montreal.

Kinka Izakaya Montreal

A restaurant that looks to present authentic Japanese dishes. Their selections include sake, craft beer, and more.

Quebec's "Ice Wine"

A unique kind of wine made from frozen grapes before fermentation. As a result, it gives the wine a more concentrated taste. It's necessary for the wine to be produced in a cold climate, thus making Canada a fitting place. This kind of wine can be found around Canada and in many restaurants.
