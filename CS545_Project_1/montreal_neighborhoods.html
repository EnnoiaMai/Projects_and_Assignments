Neighborhoods

Old Montreal

This district is most representative of Montreal's history as it dates back to the city's beginnings. The area contains many restaurants, shops, and sites that tourists should visit. The architecture of the buildings adds onto the unique "feel" of Montreal.

Place Jacques-Cartier

A square within Old Montreal, this area acts as the meeting place for many of its residents and newcomers. It can also be viewed as the entrance into Old Montreal.

Downtown Montreal

A business district in Old Montreal that houses what is known as Montreal's underground city. This "Underground City" consists of hundreds of shops and restaurants but is more of an indoor city than its literal label.

Montreal Chinatown

This neighborhood is consistent in theme to a place that offers access to many ethnic and asian foods and culture. Though called "Chinatown", this area includes several other ethnic food places such as Japanese, Korean, and Vietnamese restaurants.
